Let's Pay The Postage: Mike The Mailman Is Retiring

48 Years Ago...

48 Years ago, on April 1st, 1968, Mike Herr joined the US Postal Office.

40 Years ago, he became Penn State's very own Mailman, on the very day he was offered the job.

In his time at Penn State, Mike the Mailman has been a shining example of what it means to be a Penn Stater - a personifation of our campus.

As he puts it, “I think Penn State is the best place. Working here with so many people from different walks of life, all here trying to make it a better place, and that's what I'm trying to do."


From All Walks Of Life...

Mike has made appearances for events and intiatives from all parts of campus, with a smile and a sharp pair of shoes.

From Homecoming parades, to his very own bar tour, to the very first THON Mail Call in 1990, Mike has seen Penn State in many different lights.

But it's been from his office in the basement of McAllister Building that he's been able to make the biggest impact.

“To me, the best part has been all of the people who have walked into my office here at University Park, from the one-time visits from prospective future students to the regulars that have formed friendships with my family and I. To me, those are the best mementos.”


It's not a goodbye...

To celebrate Mike’s career, we will be turning the tables and collecting stories, pictures, and memories for Mike’s final mail call.

We will be collecting mail from 3/14 - 3/30 at the mailbox on the Information Desk in the HUB.

If you've ever had the chance to meet Mike, or he's brightened your day somehow, please feel free to drop off a letter.

As Mike as shown us over the years: small actions go a long way. Here's our opportunity to pay the postage.

On April 1st at 11:30 AM, we'll be giving Mike some mail of his own to read into his retirement.

From the entire student body, Mike, thank you so much for all you've done.
